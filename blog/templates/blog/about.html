Hello! It’s very nice to meet you! My name is Anastasia. I am a linguist and a French teacher with 5 years of experience.



Every one of my students had the same issue. They struggled to speak French. and to understand the fast French speech. DESPITE their level. Even those who had a broad vocabulary and a solid grammar foundation! That brought me to the conclusion: the root of the problem is not in grammar or vocabulary.
If you want to speak like a native, you need to learn their language like them. What do I mean by that? You must start with phonetics. When you were a child, you learned first sounds, then syllables, words, and phrases. And you succeeded in learning it! Why the heck don’t we use the same tactic with French?
I’m not the only one emphasizing this method. Look what Dr. Sulzberger has found in his research: “Neural tissue required to learn and understand a new language will develop automatically from simple exposure to the language—which is how babies learn their first language”.
